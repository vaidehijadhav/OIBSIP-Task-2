STEPHEN HAWKING

Famous physicist, professor and author

1942-2018

“However difficult life may seem, there is always something good you can succeed at.”
-Stephen Hawking

Stephen Hawking was a world-renowned theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author who made groundbreaking contributions to our understanding of the universe. He was born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England, and passed away on March 14, 2018, at the age of 76.

Early life and education

Hawking grew up in a family of intellectuals, and he showed an early aptitude for science and mathematics. He attended University College, Oxford, where he studied physics, and later earned his Ph.D. in cosmology from Trinity College, Cambridge. It was during his time at Cambridge that Hawking was diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), a progressive neurodegenerative disease that eventually left him paralyzed and unable to speak without the aid of a computerized voice synthesizer.

Contributions to Science

Despite his physical challenges, Hawking continued to make groundbreaking contributions to the field of cosmology throughout his career. His most famous work was his theory of black hole radiation, which suggested that black holes could emit energy and gradually shrink over time. This work challenged existing theories of the nature of black holes and helped lay the foundation for our current understanding of these mysterious objects.

Hawking also wrote many popular science books that made complex scientific concepts accessible to a wider audience. His most famous book, "A Brief History of Time," has sold millions of copies worldwide and is widely regarded as one of the most important scientific works of the 20th century.

Stephen Hawking's books

  • A Brief History of Time In 1988 Hawking released this informative book about the overview of space, time, and the existence of god. This became an instant best seller , selling millions of copies.
  • The Universe in a Nutshell A Brief History of Time also wasn't as easy to understand as some had hoped. So in 2001, Hawking followed up his book with The Universe in a Nutshell, which offered a more illustrated guide to cosmology's big theories.
  • The Grand Design The Grand Design was Hawking's first major publication in almost a decade. Within his new work, Hawking set out to challenge Sir Isaac Newton's belief that the universe had to have been designed by God, simply because it could not have been born from chaos. "It is not necessary to invoke God to light the blue touch paper and set the universe going," Hawking said.

Legacy and Impact

Hawking's contributions to science and popular culture were widely recognized and celebrated throughout his life. He was awarded numerous honors and awards, including the Albert Einstein Medal, the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and a knighthood from Queen Elizabeth II. In addition, he was the subject of many documentaries, books, and films, and his life and work inspired countless people around the world.

Stephen Hawking passed away on March 14, 2018, but his legacy continues to inspire future generations of scientists, thinkers, and innovators. He showed us that even in the face of seemingly insurmountable challenges, we can still make meaningful contributions to the world and make a lasting impact.
